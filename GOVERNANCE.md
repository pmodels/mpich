# MPICH Governance Policy

## Governance Model

The MPICH Project adopts a flexible hybrid governance model consisting of a
Technical Core Team and a Steering Committee.

The Technical Core Team is responsible for the day-to-day technical development
and maintenance of MPICH. Core Team members are generally those granted write
access to the MPICH GitHub repository. Core Team members may make routine
technical decisions within their areas of responsibility. While review and
discussion are strongly encouraged, the level of process required may vary
depending on the scope, urgency, and impact of a change.

The Steering Committee provides strategic guidance and community oversight. The
committee may be convened to discuss significant technical, organizational, or
policy matters affecting the project. Through the voting process defined below,
the committee may affirm, modify, or overturn decisions made by the Technical
Core Team.

The governance model is intentionally lightweight and is designed to balance
efficient project development with broad community participation.

## Steering Committee

The MPICH Steering Committee is an open committee composed of individuals with
demonstrated interest in the success of the MPICH ecosystem.

Eligibility for membership includes, but is not limited to:

- Past contributions to MPICH, including code, documentation, testing, or community support.
- Stewardship of a downstream MPICH-derived implementation.
- Ownership or maintenance of applications, tools, or software that depend substantially on MPICH.
- Other forms of significant involvement with the MPICH community.

Membership requires:

1. Nomination by an existing Steering Committee member.
2. Approval through a committee vote.

The committee may establish additional membership procedures as needed.

Committee Membership Maintenance

Steering Committee members are expected to participate in committee discussions
and votes.

A member who records three consecutive absentee votes shall be considered
inactive and may be removed from the committee. Removal under this provision
requires a special committee vote. For the purpose of such a vote, absentee
votes shall not be counted. Upon approval, the Technical Core Team shall
administratively remove the member from the committee.

## Steering Committee Voting

Any Steering Committee member may call for a vote. Votes may be conducted either
during a meeting or asynchronously through electronic communication (for
example, email).

Votes are scored as follows:

- Yes: +2
- No: −2
- No response (absentee): −1

A proposal passes if the final score is positive.

For asynchronous votes, a voting period of at least 24 hours shall be provided
before absentee votes are counted and the result is finalized.

A Steering Committee decision supersedes conflicting decisions of the Technical
Core Team.

The Steering Committee voting process is designed to require active participation
and broad support for committee actions. As the committee grows, proposals are
expected to receive support from a correspondingly larger number of members.
This design intentionally favors stability and consensus over rapid governance
decisions, while allowing the Technical Core Team to remain agile in the
day-to-day development and maintenance of the project. The governance structure
therefore balances community oversight with the flexibility needed for
continuous technical progress.
